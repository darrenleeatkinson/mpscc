Metropolitan Police Service · 999 / 111 simulation

A living command & control platform for Greater London.

From the moment British Telecom hands over a 999 call, MPSCC follows the whole journey — intake, assessment, incident creation, dispatch, and the live movement of tens of thousands of officers across the streets of London — and makes every second of it observable.

The queue ebbs and flows with a daily demand curve — busiest on Friday/Saturday evenings, quietest 23:00–06:00.

Guiding principle

The map only renders what you can see.

MPSCC never pushes 3,000 updates/sec to a browser. Clients subscribe to a viewport (bounds + zoom); the backend streams only the resources inside that box, clustered when zoomed out. That single decision is what makes the scale tractable.

Prototype & simulation. Synthetic data only. Not connected to any real emergency-service, telephony, or police system. Built to demonstrate a modern C2 experience and to generate rich Dynatrace observability telemetry.
